### **What Does “Bug” Mean?**

A **bug** is an **error, flaw, or defect in software** that causes a program to behave **incorrectly or differently than expected**.

***

### **Simple Definition (Interview-Friendly)**

> A bug is a mistake in a program that leads to incorrect results, crashes, or unexpected behavior.

***

### **Examples of Bugs**

*   An app **crashes** when you click a button
*   A login system **accepts wrong passwords**
*   A calculation gives the **wrong output**
*   A page **loads forever** due to a logic error

***

### **Common Types of Bugs**

*   **Syntax bugs** – missing semicolons, wrong keywords
*   **Logic bugs** – incorrect conditions or calculations
*   **Runtime bugs** – occur while the program is running (e.g., null pointer)
*   **UI bugs** – layout issues, broken buttons
*   **Performance bugs** – slow response, memory leaks
*   **Security bugs** – vulnerabilities that can be exploited

***

### **Why Are Bugs Important?**

*   They affect **functionality**
*   Can cause **data loss or security issues**
*   Reduce **user trust**
*   Increase **maintenance cost**

***

### **How Bugs Are Handled**

1.  **Detected** (testing, monitoring, user reports)
2.  **Reported** (bug tracking tools like Jira)
3.  **Analyzed**
4.  **Fixed**
5.  **Tested again**

***

### **Quick One‑Liner**

> A bug is an unintended error in software that causes it to work incorrectly.
